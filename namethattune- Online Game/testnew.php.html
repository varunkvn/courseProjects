Top 10 Things that you probably don't know about "Name That Tune"
#10 – The show debuted 61 years ago as one of the original game shows on television.
#9 – The original run of the series ran from 1952 through 1985.
#8 – The series ran on CBS & NBC primetime for most of its initial run.
#7 – The show has been a hit on television in the following territories: The United Kingdom, Germany, Brazil, Italy, Poland, Russia, Viet Nam, Australia, Belgium, Canada, France, Indonesia, Morocco, Turkey, the Ukraine, Slovakia, Hungary, Spain, Portugal and the United States to name a few.
#6 – In Poland, it airs seven days a week and is the equivalent of "Wheel of Fortune."
#5 – Kathie Lee Gifford, then known as Kathie Lee Johnson was discovered on "Name That Tune" as the La La Girl.
#4 – In the last two years, David Letterman, Hilary Swank, Good Morning America, Conan O'Brien, The View, Ellen DeGeneres, Juliana Margulies, Jimmy Fallon, The Today Show and about 500 radio stations have all played "Name That Tune" publicly .
#3 – The following games have all been rounds on "Name That Tune": Pick a Tune, Cassette Roulette, Melody Roulette, Build a Tune, Money Tree, Pick a Prize, Ring That Bell, Sing a Tune, Tune Countdown, Tune Topics, The Golden Medley, Tournament of Champions, Mystery Round, Mystery Guests, Decade Download, The Million Dollar Minute, and the famous Bid A Note amongst others.
#2 – "Name That Tune" was bought by Les Moonves six years ago with Donny Osmond attached to host. A pilot was ordered but never produced after Donny left the show to do "The Great American Dream Vote."
#1 – When polled, game show aficionados and fanatics will tell you that this is the #1 game they would like to see back on television. In England, 54% of their audience polled said the would watch "Name That Tune" if it was back on television.
Bonus Bonus Bonus - Over 16 million people play "Song Pop" everyday online, (which is just "who can name the song title the fastest").
